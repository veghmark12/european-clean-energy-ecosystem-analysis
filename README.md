# Mapping Europe's Clean Energy Innovation Ecosystem
Data-driven analysis of 20,000+ European scaleups and 2,500 global corporate R&amp;D investors to identify leading clean-energy innovation hubs across Europe.
## Overview

Achieving climate neutrality requires rapid deployment and commercialization of clean-energy technologies.

This project analyzes startup ecosystems, investment activity, corporate R&D spending, and regional innovation infrastructure across Europe to identify the strongest clean-energy innovation hubs.

The work was presented at the 50th Eurasian Business and Economics Society (EBES) Conference in Lisbon, Portugal. (Achieving Climate Neutrality: 
Analyzing EU Clean Energy Efforts, Innovations, and Investments)

## Research Questions

- Where are Europe's strongest clean-energy innovation hubs?
- Which regions attract the highest investment activity?
- How do universities, investors, accelerators, and startups interact within innovation ecosystems?
- What factors are associated with successful clean-energy scaleup formation?

## Data Sources

- 20,000+ European scaleups
- 2,500 largest global corporate R&D investors
- Regional innovation ecosystem data
- OECD Functional Urban Area (FUA) framework
- Clean-energy investment datasets exceeding €24 billion

## Methodology

### 1. Data Cleaning
- Standardized company metadata
- Removed duplicates
- Harmonized geographic information

### 2. NLP-Based Classification
- Classified startups based on industry and technology tags
- Identified 500+ clean-energy scaleups

### 3. Geospatial Analysis
- Mapped startups, investors, universities, and accelerators
- Applied OECD Functional Urban Area framework

### 4. Ecosystem Ranking
- Developed a composite ranking methodology
- Evaluated startup density, funding activity, research infrastructure, and ecosystem support organizations
